COLOR PALETTE — SHINY BLACK & WHITE (WITH ACCENT)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Primary palette is high-contrast monochrome. A landscaping site running
black-and-white as its UI frame stands apart from every competitor still
using earthy greens and stone everywhere. The photography carries the natural
color — the chrome does not need to compete with it.

  Deep black    #111111 / #0D0D0D  — navbar, footer, dark sections, overlays
  Pure white    #FFFFFF            — body bg, light sections, text on dark
  Accent green  #3C6E47 (approx)   — CTA buttons, nav link hover, active states
                                     NOWHERE ELSE. The restraint is the style.

No muddy mid-grays as dominant colors. If gray appears, it is near-black or
near-white only. "Shiny" means polished and intentional — clean high-contrast
photography, tight grid edges, nothing accidental or cheap.

Note: This is a deliberate shift from the original earth-tone plan. The
photography provides the warmth and natural color. The UI frame does not.


LOGO
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Two versions required:
  White version        — dark navbar, dark hero overlay, footer
  Color/dark version   — white and light section backgrounds

Rules:
  - No drop shadows on the logo, ever.
  - No glow, no gradient fill, no outline effect.
  - Clean vector on background, full stop.
  - On mobile: scale down proportionally. If the logo has both an icon and
    a wordmark, consider showing only the icon/mark in the mobile nav to
    preserve clearspace.
  - Logo does NOT watermark every page. Nav and footer only.


NAVBAR
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The navbar carries three keywords simultaneously: translucency, shiny black,
and sharp. It is the densest UI element on the site.

Behavior:
  Over hero          — fully transparent; hero photo shows behind it
  On scroll (~80px)  — transitions to frosted glass black (300ms ease)
  Position           — sticky through all scroll

Frosted glass treatment:
  background:        rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.65)
  backdrop-filter:   blur(14px)
  border-bottom:     1px solid rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.12)

The blur creates depth (page scrolling behind the nav is faintly visible).
The 1px white border is the "shiny" element — barely visible, just enough
separation to read as a polished surface.

Contents:
  Logo (left)              — white version
  Nav links (right)        — white, sharp sans-serif, no underline
  "Get a Quote" (far right) — sharp-cornered button (border-radius: 2px max),
                              filled accent green or white-outlined

Link hover state: accent green color transition. No underline ever.

Mobile behavior:
  Hamburger icon  — sharp geometry (not a soft icon)
  Tap → full-height overlay drawer, same frosted glass black
  Links stacked vertically, large tap targets
  CTA button large at bottom of drawer


TRANSLUCENCY EFFECTS — THREE LOCATIONS ONLY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Used sparingly. Translucency is a premium detail, not a surface treatment.

  1. Navbar (primary) — described above

  2. Portfolio card labels — each named project card has a frosted glass
     strip at the bottom of the photo containing only the project name.
     On hover, the strip opacity increases or the card lifts slightly.
     Format: rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.55) + backdrop-filter: blur(8px)
     NOT a solid black bar — the glass strip lets the photo read through.

  3. Hero text panel (optional) — the headline block can sit inside a
     subtle dark-glass panel for legibility without killing the photo.
     Alternative: a bottom-weighted gradient overlay on the full hero
     (linear-gradient from transparent at top to rgba(0,0,0,0.45) at
     bottom) — no explicit card shape needed.

What this is NOT:
  - Heavy frosted panels floating over body content
  - Glass on form fields or inputs
  - Translucent section backgrounds that obscure body text


TYPOGRAPHY — SHARP
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
One typeface throughout. No serif/sans mix.
Recommendation: Inter or Plus Jakarta Sans (geometric, sharp, well-hinted)

Scale:
  Eyebrow labels     12–13px, weight 500, letter-spacing: 0.14em, ALL CAPS
                     "OUR WORK" / "HOW IT WORKS" / "WHERE WE WORK"
                     Float above every section headline. All-caps = structure
                     without adding word count.
  H1 hero headline   52–72px, weight 700–800, line-height 1.05–1.10
                     3–6 words maximum. Statement, not explanation.
  H2 section heads   32–42px, weight 700
  Body               16–17px, weight 400, line-height 1.65
                     Generous for readability when text does appear — its
                     rarity gives it weight.

Rules:
  - No letter-spacing on headlines or body. Only eyebrow labels track wide.
  - No italic body text. Italics only for photo captions or light emphasis.
  - No outline fonts, no gradient text fills, no text-shadow.
  - Text is white on dark or black on white. Never gray-on-gray.


LAYOUT — SHARP, PROFESSIONAL, IMAGE FORWARD
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Grid:
  12-column, max-width ~1340px, centered. Sections respect the grid.
  No elements floating outside it without intentional reason.

Border-radius:
  Cards, images, section blocks, inputs: 0px (hard corners)
  Buttons only: 2px maximum

Shadows:
  No soft blurred drop shadows — ever.
  Hover state on cards: 1–2px sharp offset box-shadow only.
  Otherwise no shadow at all.

Section spacing:
  96–120px top/bottom padding per section.
  The white space is intentional. It is the Miracle Farms signal.
  Cramming sections together removes the premium read immediately.

Alternating dark/light sections:
  White background sections alternate with deep black sections.
  No earthy stone or beige sections — the old palette is out.
  Just white and black, alternating with purpose, letting the
  photography provide all the warmth and color in between.

Image rules:
  - Images fill containers edge to edge. No inner padding, no border,
    no border-radius on images.
  - Project portfolio cards: the photo IS the card. Full bleed,
    aspect-ratio locked (4:3 or 3:2). Frosted label strip at bottom.
  - Hero: 100vh, object-fit: cover, center-weighted crop. ONE photo.
    No carousel, no slider. One dominant image per page.
  - Service tiles: cropped category photos, dark overlay, icon + label
    only — no description text in the tile.
  - Icons: line-weight (1.5px stroke), geometric, consistent sizing.
    Not soft filled icons.

Copy budget (enforced):
  Hero:              3–5 word headline + 1 subline + 2 CTA buttons. Done.
  Section headline:  1 eyebrow label + 1 headline (6 words max) + optional
                     1–2 sentence subhead. No paragraphs in section intros.
  Service pages:     2–3 sentences per service. That is the entire page body.
  Project cards:     Project name only. The photo explains the rest.
  Homepage:          Zero body copy above the fold. Ever.


SUMMARY — THE SIGNAL THIS SITE SENDS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Every other DFW landscaping website uses green, stone beige, and paragraph
copy to explain their services. This site uses black, white, and photography
to show them. The contrast — visual and strategic — is the competitive
positioning. A visitor landing here should feel like they found the premium
option without reading a single word.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
TYPOGRAPHY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Typeface decision (choose one, not both):
  OPTION A: Inter — The safer choice. Universally legible, excellent at all
    weights, renders perfectly on every OS, very well-hinted for small sizes.
    Google Fonts, free, no license concern. The most "neutral sharp" option.

  OPTION B: Plus Jakarta Sans — Slightly more character than Inter. Has a
    small degree of personality in its uppercase letterforms without being
    loud. Also Google Fonts, free. Recommended if Inter feels too utilitarian
    once you see it composited with the photography.

  DO NOT MIX typefaces. One family, all weights, everywhere. The discipline
  of a single typeface is part of the professional signal.

  Fallback stack (CSS):
    font-family: 'Plus Jakarta Sans', 'Inter', system-ui, -apple-system,
                 BlinkMacSystemFont, 'Segoe UI', sans-serif;

Weights used:
  400   Body copy, captions, nav links (default)
  500   Eyebrow labels, secondary CTAs, subheadings
  600   Button labels, trust strip stats
  700   Section headlines (H2), card titles
  800   Hero headline (H1) only

Scale — desktop (clamps to mobile values below 768px):
  --text-eyebrow    12px / 13px, weight 500, letter-spacing: 0.14em, uppercase
  --text-xs         13px, weight 400, line-height: 1.5   (captions, meta)
  --text-sm         15px, weight 400, line-height: 1.6   (form labels, tags)
  --text-base       16px, weight 400, line-height: 1.65  (body copy)
  --text-md         18px, weight 500, line-height: 1.55  (intro subheads)
  --text-lg         22px, weight 500, line-height: 1.4   (card titles)
  --text-xl         28px, weight 700, line-height: 1.25  (H3, sub-section heads)
  --text-2xl        38px, weight 700, line-height: 1.15  (H2, section headlines)
  --text-3xl        52px, weight 800, line-height: 1.05  (H1 hero — desktop)
  --text-hero       clamp(40px, 6vw, 72px)               (H1 — fluid/responsive)

Mobile scale (max-width: 767px):
  --text-2xl        28px  (H2 collapses to fit single-column)
  --text-hero       clamp(36px, 10vw, 52px)

Rules that do not change at any breakpoint:
  - Eyebrow labels: always uppercase, always 0.14em letter-spacing, always 12–13px
  - Headlines: never italic, never underlined, never gradient-filled
  - Body: never all-caps, never letter-spacing, never centered on desktop
    (centered body text is allowed on mobile in a 1-column layout only)
  - No text-shadow anywhere. Not even 1px on hero photography.
    (Legibility is handled by the overlay/gradient behind the text, not shadows)

SPACING SYSTEM — 8PX BASE GRID
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
All spacing is a multiple of 8px. This is enforced for padding, margin, gap,
and border-width. No 5px, no 10px, no 15px. Use 8, 16, 24, 32, 40, 48, 56,
64, 80, 96, 112, 128 only.

  --space-1    8px     Tight internal padding (button label padding-top/bottom)
  --space-2    16px    Default component gap, internal card padding
  --space-3    24px    Nav height padding, form field spacing
  --space-4    32px    Between elements within a section
  --space-5    40px    Mobile section padding top/bottom
  --space-6    48px    Gap between section headline and content
  --space-8    64px    Between major subsections within a page
  --space-10   80px    Section vertical padding on tablet
  --space-12   96px    Section vertical padding — desktop default
  --space-16   128px   Hero text vertical offset from top

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
LAYOUT GRID
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Max content width:   1340px  (wider than typical 1200px — gives photos room)
  Column count:        12
  Column gutter:       24px (desktop); 16px (tablet); 0 (mobile — full bleed)
  Layout margins:      auto (centered)
  Mobile margin:       20px left/right (not 16 — gives just a bit more breath)

Key breakpoints:
  Mobile    <768px    Single column, full bleed images, hamburger nav
  Tablet    768–1023px  2-column, reduced hero text, portrait orientation ok
  Desktop   1024px+   Full 12-column, all components in final form
  Wide      1440px+   Max-width container, background fills stretch to edge

Full-bleed rule:
  Images, hero sections, and the dark alternating sections break OUT of the
  max-width container and fill the full viewport width. The content inside
  those sections still respects the 1340px max-width. The visual edge-to-edge
  is what creates the magazine layout quality.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
BORDER, RADIUS & SHADOW
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Radius:
  --radius-none    0px    Cards, images, section blocks, inputs, textareas
  --radius-btn     2px    Buttons only (barely there — preserves "sharp" feel)
  --radius-badge   2px    Trust strip badges and credential tags

Shadows:
  Default:         none   No drop shadows anywhere in resting state
  Card hover:      0 2px 0 0 rgba(0,0,0,0.8)   Sharp 2px bottom offset —
                   this reads as the card "pressing" slightly rather than
                   floating. Evokes physicality, not softness.
  Button hover:    inset 0 -2px 0 rgba(0,0,0,0.25)   Subtle pressed-in feel
  Image:           none — images never get shadows
  Navbar:          none — the glass border handles the separation

Borders:
  Light section dividers:   1px solid #E0E0E0 (color-gray-200)
  Dark section dividers:    1px solid rgba(255,255,255,0.12)
  Focus ring (inputs):      2px solid #3C6E47 (accent), no box-shadow
                            (do not use browser default blue outline)

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
MOTION & ANIMATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This site moves slowly and deliberately. Fast animations read as cheap.
Every transition should feel like something heavy and intentional settling
into place.

Global easing:
  --ease-default    cubic-bezier(0.25, 0.0, 0.25, 1.0)   Most transitions
  --ease-in-out     cubic-bezier(0.4, 0.0, 0.2, 1.0)     Menu opens/closes
  --ease-out        cubic-bezier(0.0, 0.0, 0.25, 1.0)    Elements entering view

Durations:
  --dur-fast        150ms   Hover color changes (text, icon color)
  --dur-default     250ms   Button bg fills, border color
  --dur-medium      300ms   Navbar glass transition on scroll
  --dur-slow        500ms   Menu drawer open/close
  --dur-reveal      700ms   On-scroll section entry (see below)

DO NOT use:
  - Bounce or spring easing (too playful)
  - Infinite spin or pulse animations (never)
  - Parallax scrolling (high CPU cost, questionable UX value)
  - Auto-playing hero video (kills mobile battery, slows Core Web Vitals)

On-scroll entry animation:
  Elements animate in once as they enter the viewport.
  Pattern: opacity 0→1 + translateY 24px→0 over 700ms ease-out
  Stagger: 100ms between items in a row (e.g., trust strip badges stagger 1,2,3,4)
  Triggered by IntersectionObserver at 15% visibility threshold.
  Does NOT re-animate on scroll back up.

Rotating hero headline (if used):
  Fade between phrases: opacity 0→1→hold→1→0, 3000ms hold per phrase
  No slide or wipe — just a clean cross-fade
  Implementation: CSS animation or lightweight JS, no library needed

6. SECTION ANATOMY — THE REPEATING PATTERN
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Every non-hero section on the site follows the same heading structure:

  [EYEBROW LABEL]         — 12–13px, weight 500, uppercase, 0.14em tracking
                            accent green on white sections
                            rgba(255,255,255,0.55) on black sections
  [SECTION HEADLINE]      — --text-2xl, weight 700, black or white
  [OPTIONAL SUBHEAD]      — --text-md, weight 400, secondary text color
                            1–2 sentences ONLY. If it requires 3, cut it.

Spacing from headline to subhead: 14px
Spacing from subhead to content:  40–48px
Section top/bottom padding:       96px (desktop); 64px (tablet); 48px (mobile)

This structure applies to EVERY content section. The rhythm of seeing this
same pattern creates a sense of order and professionalism even before the
content reads.

PHOTOGRAPHY DIRECTION
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The aesthetic lives or dies on photo quality. Every CSS decision above becomes
irrelevant if the photography is weak. This section defines what a qualifying
photo looks like versus what gets cut.

HERO PHOTO REQUIREMENTS (full-viewport use)
  - Native resolution minimum: 1920px wide (ideally 2400px+)
  - Aspect ratio: naturally 16:9 or wider (landscape orientation)
  - Depth of field: shallow is not required, but the subject must be clear
  - Subject: the work — a finished installation, not process shots
  - Lighting: golden hour or overcast (not harsh midday — creates blown
    highlights and deep shadows that photograph poorly at full viewport)
  - Sky/background: a visible sky or natural backdrop is better than a fence
  - People: no people visible (timeless vs. dated)
  - Equipment: no tools, hoses, wheelbarrows in frame
  - Must hold up at any crop point (center-left, center, center-right)
    — test the photo at a 12:5 crop before committing

HERO BEST CANDIDATES from the scraped library:
  (These need to be confirmed at full resolution — these are descriptive,
   not guarantees)
  water-features/    Spill-Over-Pottery-Water-Feature-with-Magnolias images
                     Rock-Swimming-Pool-Waterfall.jpg
  outdoor-living/    Flagstone-Pathway-Pergola-Gazebo.jpg
                     Stained-Concrete-Gazebo-Outdoor-Living.jpg
  landscaping/       Colorful-Landscape.jpg
  lighting/          Any nighttime-lit garden shot — premium feel, rare in
                     the DFW landscaping niche, immediately differentiating

INTERIOR PAGE HERO (service pages, about, etc.)
  - Same quality requirements but can be tighter crop / portrait-forward
  - 1200px wide minimum
  - These use a shorter hero: 50–60vh instead of 100vh

WHAT GETS CUT:
  - Any image with a watermark, logo bug, or overlay text from another source
  - Any image showing Facebook UI (cropped social export)
  - Any image named with a Facebook ID pattern (1049745...) — these are
    typically mobile phone exports, rarely hero quality
  - Any image where the primary subject is out-of-focus relative to the frame
  - Square photos (they cannot fill a landscape hero without bizarre cropping)
  - Screenshots or composite images

PHOTOGRAPHY DIRECTION IF A SHOOT IS PLANNED:
  - Shoot in golden hour (1 hour post-sunrise or 1–2 hrs pre-sunset)
  - Bring two types of shots per project:
    1. Wide establishing shot: the whole installation in its environment
    2. Detail shots: texture of stone, water motion, foliage pattern
  - Shoot both with and without sun flare (useful for warm/cool variations)
  - Target 3–5 hero-quality wide shots; 10–15 detail shots per project
  - RAW files preferred; provide full-resolution JPGs at delivery
  - Do NOT over-edit: no HDR halos, no saturation pumping, no vignettes
    The editing goal is "as if you were there on a beautiful day" — not a
    real estate photo or social media post.

WHAT THIS DESIGN IS NOT
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
These are the specific failure modes to avoid — things that would dilute the
aesthetic or undermine the premium signal:

  ✕ Earth tones and beige as section backgrounds
    The natural warmth comes from the photos. The UI frame stays cold and sharp.

  ✕ Rounded corners on cards and images
    Softness signals consumer tech (mobile apps, social media). Not a premium
    residential contractor. Hard edges are the design language.

  ✕ Drop shadows on images or cards (the soft, blurred kind)
    Shadows make things look like they are floating. That is not the feeling.
    Physicality comes from the sharp offset shadow on hover only.

  ✕ Gradient fills on buttons
    A gradient button in 2026 reads as a template site. Flat fill, full stop.

  ✕ Stock photography
    If there are not enough hero-quality photos from the real portfolio, plan
    a shoot. A stock photo of grass and a generic pergola is immediately read
    as false by any local client. It destroys trust.

  ✕ Any animation that loops or auto-plays without user intent
    No rotating banner carousels. No auto-advancing image sliders. No pulsing
    badge animations. Motion is for transitions and reveals, not decoration.

  ✕ The phrase "Welcome to our website" or any equivalent
    The site does not explain that it is a website.

  ✕ Showing 8 navigation items
    The simplified nav (6 links) is not optional — it follows from the
    "less words" brief. Every item in the nav is a commitment.

  ✕ Sidebar layouts
    The current site has a sidebar. The new site does not. Sidebars are for
    blogs with lots of content and internal links to manage. The new site's
    focus is singular on every page.

  ✕ Paragraph copy in the hero
    There is exactly one acceptable exception: a single subline (~12 words).
    Nothing else belongs above the CTAs in the hero.

  ✕ Mixing the accent green into decorative contexts
    The green is a functional color: CTA buttons, hover, active states.
    If it starts appearing in decorative dividers, icon fills, section
    accents, or background tints, the "sharp monochrome with accent" palette
    collapses into standard landscaping-green.
