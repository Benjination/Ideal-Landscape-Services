SECTION 1 — CORE DESIGN PHILOSOPHY
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

THE CLIENT'S BRIEF (Kathy's exact words)
  "We just want more aesthetics and less words. Our current designer wants to
  keep in a whole bunch of redundant repetitive words that they say help
  Optimize the Google engine… I just feel like we could do that without so
  much redundancy."

THE PRINCIPLE
  Photography IS the content. Every page should be led by a strong image —
  text is supporting caption, not primary message. The goal is a site that
  feels like a curated landscape portfolio magazine, not a service pamphlet.

  This does NOT mean ignoring SEO. It means doing SEO right: through page
  structure, semantic HTML, metadata, image alt text, named section headings,
  a service area section, and a live review stream — not through repeated
  body copy stuffing the same keywords six times per page.

THE BLEND (Primary: Miracle Farms aesthetics + Secondary: Worx UI patterns)
  - Photography-forward, editorial layout (Miracle Farms)
  - Smart trust signals and process clarity (Worx)
  - Copy budget: 1 headline + 1–2 sentences MAX per section
  - White space and imagery convey quality better than copy
  - All-caps eyebrow labels ("OUR WORK", "HOW IT WORKS") add structure
    without adding words

TYPOGRAPHIC VOICE
  - Headlines: Large, bold, short. 3–6 words. Statement, not explanation.
  - Body: Sparse. When text appears, it lands with weight because it is rare.
  - Tagline candidate: "Design — Build — Maintain" (Miracle Farms pattern)
    or "Your Ideal Outdoors, Built." — confirm with David.
  - All-caps sparingly as ambient texture, never as body text.

COLOR DIRECTION
  - Palette: Earthy neutrals, warm stone tones, deep greens — nothing loud.
  - Dark photo sections alternate with white/light sections (visual rhythm).
  - Single accent color for CTAs (lean green or warm amber — TBD).
  - High contrast: white text on dark photos; dark text on light sections.

SECTION 4 — SEO STRATEGY (Visual-First, No Verbosity)
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The client's concern was valid: keyword stuffing is outdated. Google's current
ranking signals reward relevance, structure, and authority — not repetition.
Here's how to achieve strong SEO without writing a wall of copy:

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
4A. ON-PAGE STRUCTURE (the foundation)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  [ ] One clear H1 per page — unique, keyword-present, human-readable.
      Example homepage H1: "Landscape Design & Outdoor Living — Dallas/Fort Worth"
  [ ] H2/H3 hierarchy used for every section heading (not just styled divs)
      → Google reads these as content signals even if visually they look
        like decorative section labels.
  [ ] Semantic HTML throughout: <section>, <article>, <nav>, <header>,
      <footer>, <address> — helps crawlers understand page structure.
  [ ] Each service page has its OWN URL slug:
      /services/water-features/, /services/hardscapes/, /services/lighting/
      → Separate URLs = separate keyword targets = more search real estate

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
4B. IMAGE ALT TEXT (probably the biggest quick win)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Every image needs a descriptive, keyword-natural alt attribute. This is where
  the visual-first site earns its SEO — the images ARE the content, so the
  alt text IS the copy. Examples:
    Bad:  alt="img_0042.jpg"
    Good: alt="Flagstone patio with cedar pergola and outdoor kitchen, Allen TX"
    Good: alt="Spill-over pottery water feature with Japanese maples, Fort Worth"
    Good: alt="Landscape lighting installation on residential property, Frisco TX"
  
  Rule of thumb: describe what's in the photo + mention a DFW city name where
  it fits naturally. Every single image in the gallery should have this.
  Across 400+ images, this alone is a significant SEO signal.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
4C. META TITLES & DESCRIPTIONS (per page)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Every page needs a unique, keyword-rich meta title and description.
  These appear in Google search results — they are ALSO ad copy.
  
  Examples:
    Homepage:
      Title: "Ideal Landscape Services | Landscape Design & Outdoor Living — DFW"
      Desc:  "Award-winning landscape design, water features, hardscapes, and
              outdoor living spaces in the Dallas–Fort Worth area. Family-owned,
              35+ years. Call for a FREE estimate."
    
    Water Features page:
      Title: "Water Features & Fountains | Ideal Landscape Services | DFW"
      Desc:  "Custom water features, fountains, and ponds designed and installed
              in Dallas, Fort Worth, Allen, Plano, and surrounding DFW cities."
    
    Projects page:
      Title: "Landscape Portfolio | Our Work | Ideal Landscape Services"
      Desc:  "See completed landscape design projects across the DFW metroplex —
              patios, water features, outdoor living spaces, and more."

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
4D. SERVICE AREA SECTION / LOCAL SEO
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  The "Where We Work" section on the homepage pulls double duty:
  - Visually: A clean map embed or illustrated area graphic
  - SEO: City names as anchor links pointing to thin city subpages OR
    as plain visible text that Google can index as location signals.
  
  City subpages (optional but high-value for local SEO):
    /service-area/dallas/ — "Landscape design in Dallas, TX"
    /service-area/fort-worth/ — "Landscape design in Fort Worth, TX"
    /service-area/allen/ — "Landscape design in Allen, TX"
    etc. for each major service city in DFW
  
  Each city page: same template, 2–3 sentences swapping in the city name,
  link to services. Very low effort, high local SEO payoff.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
4E. GOOGLE BUSINESS PROFILE (GBP)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  [ ] Verify Ideal's Google Business Profile is claimed and up to date.
  [ ] Ensure NAP (Name, Address, Phone) on the website EXACTLY matches GBP.
      Any mismatch hurts local rankings.
  [ ] Add all service categories in GBP that match the site's service pages.
  [ ] Upload best photos directly to GBP — these appear in Google Maps
      results and are separate from the website.
  [ ] Encourage satisfied clients to leave Google reviews — each review
      boosts local pack ranking and feeds the live review widget on the site.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
4F. BLOG / CONTENT STRATEGY (long-tail SEO)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  The blog is where deeper keyword targeting lives — completely separate from
  service page copy. Suggested post topics for DFW landscaping SEO:
    - "Best Low-Maintenance Plants for DFW Summers"
    - "How Much Does a Backyard Water Feature Cost in Dallas?"
    - "Top 5 Landscape Design Trends in Fort Worth for 2026"
    - "When to Scalp Your Lawn in Texas (And Why It Matters)"
    - "Hardscape vs. Softscape: What's Right for a North Texas Backyard?"
    - "How to Choose a Licensed Landscape Contractor in DFW"
  Each post: one targeted topic, one header image (from the image library),
  300–600 words — meaty enough for Google, short enough to be readable.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
4G. TECHNICAL SEO CHECKLIST
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  [ ] XML sitemap auto-generated and submitted to Google Search Console
  [ ] robots.txt: allow all content pages, disallow /admin/
  [ ] Canonical URLs set on all pages (prevents duplicate content issues)
  [ ] Open Graph + Twitter Card meta tags (social sharing previews)
  [ ] Page speed: images served in WebP format, lazy-loaded below the fold
  [ ] Core Web Vitals: LCP (Largest Contentful Paint) target < 2.5s
      → Hero image must be preloaded or served from a CDN
  [ ] Schema markup: LocalBusiness schema on homepage with NAP, hours,
      service area, image, reviews — structured data Google reads directly
  [ ] 301 redirects: map all old URLs to new ones at launch so no link equity
      is lost from current site (especially any blog posts that rank)
  [ ] HTTPS enforced (auto with Firebase Hosting or most managed WP hosts)
  [ ] Google Analytics 4 (GA4) + Search Console verified before launch
